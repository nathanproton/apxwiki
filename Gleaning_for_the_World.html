    Gleaning for the World, Inc. (GFTW) is a faith-based humanitarian organization headquartered in Appomattox County, Virginia, specializing in the collection and distribution of donated medical supplies, food, and goods to people in need domestically and internationally. Founded in July 1998 by Rev. Ron Davidson, GFTW has become one of the most efficient and highly-regarded charities in the United States, having distributed over $500 million in supplies to more than 57 countries across five continents.[1][2] The organization operates from a substantial warehouse facility on Concord Turnpike and has earned recognition from Forbes magazine for its financial efficiency and charitable commitment, appearing on the magazine's top charities list multiple consecutive years.[3]

    Founding and Early History

    Gleaning for the World was founded in July 1998 by Rev. Ron Davidson, who had recently resigned from his position as pastor of a 1,200-member church.[1] The organization began modestly in a basement apartment, operating with minimal resources but a clear vision to assist impoverished people around the globe.[4] In its earliest years, GFTW collected medical supplies, food, clothing, and other needed items from donors and coordinated their distribution to communities in crisis or chronic need.[5] The organization's founding mission was grounded in faith-based humanitarian principles, motivated by a desire to serve those suffering from poverty, disease, and disaster.

    The rapid growth of GFTW in its first few years was extraordinary. Within just six years of its founding—by 2004—the organization had expanded from its basement origins to occupy an 18,000-square-foot office and warehouse facility.[6] This expansion reflected both the organization's success in fundraising and the enormous demand for humanitarian aid in disaster-affected regions and impoverished communities worldwide. The volume of supplies processed and distributed increased dramatically, establishing GFTW as a significant player in the charitable sector.

    Mission and Operations

    The core mission of Gleaning for the World is to collect donated supplies—primarily medical supplies, food, clothing, shoes, hygiene items, baby supplies, and other essential goods—and distribute them to people in need throughout the world and across the United States.[7] GFTW partners with hospitals, medical facilities, churches, schools, corporations, and individual donors to procure supplies that would otherwise be discarded or underutilized. The organization then coordinates the logistics of packing, shipping, and delivering these materials to partner organizations in destination countries and disaster-affected regions.

    By 2011, GFTW maintained an operating model based on 100 percent efficiency, meaning that virtually all donated funds go directly toward purchasing or shipping supplies rather than administrative overhead.[8] This commitment to efficiency has been central to the organization's identity and has earned it sustained recognition from charity watchdog organizations. Donors are assured that every dollar donated enables approximately $211 worth of supplies to be purchased and distributed, reflecting the organization's ability to leverage donations through partnerships with suppliers and shipping partners.[9]

    GFTW's shipments typically consist of full tractor-trailer loads sent via overland logistics. By 2014, the organization was sending multiple shipments monthly to destinations across five continents, reaching more than 54 countries.[10] Common destinations for shipments have included Latin America, the Caribbean, the Middle East, Southeast Asia, and Africa, as well as disaster zones in the United States.

    Facilities and Expansion

    Gleaning for the World's headquarters are located at 7539 Stage Road, Concord, Virginia, property that was once used as cow pasture before being developed into a significant humanitarian logistics hub.[11] The organization's facility expansion has been ongoing and substantial. Following its initial 18,000-square-foot facility in 2004, GFTW continued to expand to meet growing demand.

    By approximately 2006, the organization had approximately tripled the size of its warehouse facility, reflecting both the success of its fundraising efforts and the exponential growth in supply shipments.[12] In 2014, GFTW moved its supplies to a new warehouse on Old Courthouse Road and announced plans to expand to a 38,000-square-foot facility to further increase its operational capacity.[13] The organization also continued planning for additional building construction, including a 12,000-square-foot volunteer center that broke ground in November 2014 with completion anticipated by May 2015.[14]

    As of 2015, GFTW had opened a new volunteer center in Concord designed to increase its volunteer base from approximately 2,100 to 4,000 annually, further expanding the organization's capacity for processing and preparing shipments.[15] With these expanded facilities, the organization aimed to triple its overall productivity in the years following 2015, with President Ron Davidson calling for increased humanitarian response to rising global suffering.[16] Plans for three additional buildings were announced in 2015 to support this expansion.

    Founder and Leadership

    Rev. Ron Davidson founded Gleaning for the World after a period of spiritual and professional reorientation. Having served as pastor of a large church with approximately 1,200 members, Davidson made the decision to step away from traditional pastoral ministry to pursue full-time humanitarian work.[1] His commitment to faith-based service and compassion for the world's poor motivated the creation of GFTW, which was designed to mobilize resources and volunteers to address human suffering on a global scale.

    Davidson's leadership has been recognized by state and federal officials. In March 2014, Virginia Governor Terry McAuliffe sent a congratulatory letter to Davidson, expressing gratitude for GFTW's work.[17] Senator Tim Kaine also sent a letter of support to Davidson at the end of March 2014, commending his leadership and vision for the organization.[18]

    As President of Gleaning for the World, Davidson has shaped the organization's strategic direction and maintained its commitment to faith-based humanitarian service. He has been actively involved in assessing disaster situations firsthand, including traveling to Nepal in 2015 where he characterized the earthquake as the most difficult disaster the organization had encountered due to the staggering number of casualties.[19] Under Davidson's leadership, GFTW has grown from a basement operation to a major international humanitarian organization.

    Major Disaster Responses

    Gleaning for the World has become known for its rapid and substantial response to natural disasters and humanitarian crises around the world. The organization's ability to mobilize resources, coordinate volunteers, and ship supplies within days of a disaster has earned it recognition as a critical partner in disaster relief efforts.

    2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami

    The 2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami that devastated Southeast Asia represented one of the earliest major international responses coordinated by GFTW. The organization mobilized resources and shipped substantial quantities of relief supplies to affected countries, including food, water, medical supplies, and other essential items needed by survivors. This response established GFTW as a credible participant in international humanitarian relief.

    Hurricane Katrina and Domestic Disasters

    Gleaning for the World responded significantly to Hurricane Katrina in 2005, providing aid to affected communities along the Gulf Coast. The organization sent multiple truckloads of supplies including water, food, blankets, cleaning supplies, and other relief items to Louisiana and neighboring states.[20] This domestic disaster response demonstrated GFTW's ability to mobilize quickly for crises within the United States.

    In the years following Hurricane Katrina, GFTW responded to numerous other U.S. disasters. In 2010, the organization provided assistance to victims of flooding in Alabama, Mississippi, and Tennessee, as well as Guatemala.[21] By 2012, GFTW had developed such operational capacity that it could send four truckloads of supplies to New Orleans in response to Hurricane Isaac, including water, blankets, baby items, pet supplies, and cleaning items.[22] The organization also collected Hurricane Sandy relief supplies in 2012, demonstrating its sustained commitment to domestic disaster response.

    2008 Myanmar Cyclone

    In 2008, GFTW dispatched a significant shipment of aid to Myanmar following a deadly cyclone that caused thousands of fatalities in Southeast Asia.[23] This response highlighted the organization's global reach and its ability to coordinate international logistics to reach remote disaster zones.

    2010 Haiti Earthquake

    The January 2010 earthquake that devastated Haiti prompted one of GFTW's major international relief campaigns. The organization immediately began accepting donations and coordinating shipments of food, water, medical supplies, and other essential items to Haiti. In June 2010, the organization reached its 2,000th supply shipment milestone with a shipment of bottled water destined for flood victims in Tennessee, demonstrating the cumulative impact of its relief efforts.[24]

    GFTW continued to provide ongoing assistance to Haiti following the earthquake. The organization was actively fundraising and coordinating relief efforts months after the initial disaster, recognizing that recovery would require sustained support. In March 2011, GFTW requested donations to aid Japan earthquake and tsunami victims while maintaining ongoing operations supporting Haiti and other regions in crisis.[25]

    Recent Disaster Relief

    In 2015, Gleaning for the World responded to a massive 7.8 magnitude earthquake in Nepal and Syria, with President Ron Davidson characterizing the Nepal earthquake as exceptionally challenging due to the extraordinary number of casualties and the difficulty of establishing relief operations in affected areas.[19] The organization coordinated with on-the-ground partners to distribute thousands of meals in Kathmandu despite damaged infrastructure.[26]

    GFTW's disaster response capabilities have continued to expand. In 2016, the organization responded to Hurricane Matthew by providing aid in the United States, with distribution centers in North Carolina, South Carolina, and Florida, supplying water, hot meals, chainsaws, generators, and protective gear.[27] The organization also sent significant food and water supplies to Haiti in the wake of Hurricane Matthew.[28]

    In February 2016, when a tornado devastated parts of Appomattox County, GFTW provided immediate assistance to local victims, delivering a trailer full of supplies to the emergency shelter within four hours of its setup.[29] Wal-Mart assisted GFTW in this response by filling GFTW's trucks with water, food, and blankets for affected residents.[30]

    More recently, in 2022, GFTW responded to the war in Ukraine by receiving donated supplies at collection sites such as Sam's Club in Lynchburg and coordinating distribution of essential items to Ukrainian nationals affected by the conflict.[31] Pastor Ivanets and his brother Sascha, both native Ukrainians, assisted GFTW in distributing supplies to Ukrainian refugees.[32]

    In 2023, GFTW collected disaster relief donations for tornado victims in Mississippi and began accepting donations to aid victims of the Turkey-Syria earthquake.[33][34] In 2024, the organization coordinated with libraries and retail locations to collect donations for Hurricane Helene relief efforts, maintaining its decades-long commitment to rapid disaster response.

    Recognition and Awards

    Gleaning for the World has received significant recognition for its efficiency and charitable commitment from national organizations and public officials. Most notably, the organization has been repeatedly recognized by Forbes magazine, one of the most prestigious and rigorous evaluators of charitable organizations in the United States.

    In 2011, Gleaning for the World was ranked No. 1 by Forbes as the best charity in the nation for its charitable contributions and for giving 100 percent of its donations to those in need.[35] This recognition came as the third consecutive year that GFTW had received a top-tier award from Forbes.[36] The organization was selected from over 200 charities evaluated in Forbes's annual charity study and was featured in the December 2011 issue of Forbes Magazine, distributed on December 18, 2011.[37]

    Forbes's recognition of GFTW extended beyond 2011. In 2008, Forbes.com ranked Gleaning for the World as a top charity in the country for the second time due to its efficiency and charitable commitment.[38] The organization has maintained this recognition in subsequent years as well, continuing to appear among the most efficient charities in America.

    Beyond Forbes recognition, GFTW has been praised by state and federal officials. In March 2014, Virginia Governor Terry McAuliffe sent a congratulatory letter expressing his appreciation for the organization's humanitarian work.[17] U.S. Senator Tim Kaine similarly commended the organization's leadership and vision in a letter sent in late March 2014.[18]

    Local government officials have also recognized GFTW's contributions. In July 2014, Appomattox County Supervisor Ronnie Spiggle praised Gleaning for the World during a county board meeting and requested that the county administrator prepare a resolution supporting GFTW's Tobacco Commission grant application, reflecting the organization's importance to the local community.[39] In 2024, GFTW received competitive grant funding from the Greater Lynchburg Community Foundation, further validating the organization's standing in the philanthropic community.[40]

    Signature Programs

    Teddy Bear Brigade

    One of Gleaning for the World's most beloved and recognizable programs is the Teddy Bear Brigade, which collects new and gently used stuffed animals for distribution to orphans, refugees, abandoned children, and other vulnerable youth in crisis situations.[41] The program provides emotional comfort to children experiencing trauma, displacement, or deprivation. By 2011, the Teddy Bear Brigade had collected approximately 300,000 stuffed animals since its inception around 2001.[41]

    The Teddy Bear Brigade has become a community-wide initiative. In November 2011, Appomattox schools launched a stuffed animal collection drive to support the program, engaging students in humanitarian service.[42] The program has inspired various community events, including a Teddy Bear Parade held during the Appomattox Railroad Festival in October, which invites children to participate with their teddy bears, with donations encouraged for GFTW's Teddy Bear Brigade.[43]

    Schools, churches, and community organizations regularly participate in Teddy Bear drives. In 2013, Cornerstone Christian Academy's Family, Career and Community Leaders (FCCLA) partnered with GFTW to host a Teddy Bear Brigade stuffed animal drive.[44]

    Million Pounds Project

    The Million Pounds Project was a yearlong initiative that began in July 2010 with the ambitious goal of sending one million pounds of food to local food banks. The project successfully met its goal by July 2011, demonstrating GFTW's commitment to addressing food insecurity in the local region as well as internationally.[45] This initiative was supported by grants from local businesses; Tiger Fuel provided a $3,250 grant to support the Million Pounds Project.[46]

    Community Partnerships

    Gleaning for the World maintains extensive partnerships with local churches, schools, civic organizations, and businesses throughout Appomattox County and the broader Central Virginia region. These partnerships are essential to GFTW's operations, providing both volunteer support and supply donations.

    Local churches have been particularly engaged partners. The Woman's Missionary Union (WMU) of Rocks Baptist Church has been active in supporting GFTW since at least 2015, with the WMU regularly supporting the organization's efforts alongside other missionary activities.[47] Memorial United Methodist Church's missions committee has also provided support to GFTW.[48] New Hope Baptist Church in Vera has hosted Vacation Bible School and family events with fundraising benefiting GFTW.[49]

    Local schools have engaged with GFTW through various fundraising and service initiatives. Students at Appomattox County schools have participated in Teddy Bear Brigade collection drives, water bottle flipping contests, and other activities to raise funds and collect supplies for GFTW. The Appomattox Christian Academy's FCCLA chapter has partnered with GFTW on multiple occasions.[44]

    Major retailers have also partnered with GFTW for disaster relief and ongoing support. Wal-Mart has partnered with GFTW on multiple occasions to collect food for individuals in need, most notably for a December 2015 holiday food drive.[50] Wal-Mart also filled GFTW's trucks with supplies following the 2016 Appomattox tornado.[30] Sam's Club has regularly hosted GFTW collection drives at its Lynchburg location for various disaster relief efforts and ongoing supply collection.

    GFTW has also partnered with Jamerson Memorial Library, which has served as a drop-off location for donations in support of disaster relief efforts, including Hurricane Helene relief in 2024.[51] The organization's warehouse has even served as a temporary polling location, demonstrating its importance to the community infrastructure; in September 2020, the Stonewall voting precinct was temporarily relocated to the GFTW facility.[52]

    Community groups have also engaged in creative fundraising for GFTW. The Appomattox LEO Club, sponsored by the Appomattox LIONs and Appomattox Christian Academy, hosted a "Water Bottle Flipping Contest" on April 2, 2017, as a fundraiser for GFTW, raising money and collecting water bottles for the organization.[53]

    Fundraising and Community Engagement

    Gleaning for the World has developed multiple revenue streams and community engagement strategies to sustain its operations and expand its humanitarian reach. In addition to direct monetary donations and supply donations from individuals and businesses, GFTW conducts organized fundraising events and cultivates major donor relationships.

    Golf Tournaments

    Beginning in 2020, GFTW launched an annual Golf Classic fundraiser at Falling River Country Club in Appomattox. The Inaugural Golf Classic in October 2020 featured sixty-four golfers and included prizes for teams placing first and second, as well as special awards for "Closest to the Pin" and a "$10,000 Hole in One" award.[54] Multiple local and area businesses sponsored the event, including Virginia MetalFab, Mills Heating and Air, Dodson Pest Control, and Robinson Funeral Home.[55] The golf tournament became an annual tradition, with GFTW hosting a Second Annual Golf Classic at Falling River Country Club in October 2021.[56]

    Community Collection Drives

    GFTW regularly organizes supply collection drives at retail locations such as Sam's Club in Lynchburg and Kroger in Forest. For major disasters, the organization mobilizes volunteers and retail partners to collect supplies within days. For example, following the April 2018 tornado activity in Central Virginia, GFTW collected supplies at multiple locations, requesting donations of baby supplies, personal care items, paper products, and household cleaning supplies, and accepting supplies at its warehouse on Stage Road through April 20, 2018.[57][58]

    Online Donations

    GFTW accepts online donations through its website at gftw.org/donate, allowing donors to designate their contributions for specific disaster relief efforts or ongoing operations.[59] Donors can include comments indicating the intended use of their donation, such as typing "Fire" to designate contributions for wildfire relief.[60] The organization also accepts text donations; supporters can text AID to 859444 to make donations.[61]

    Blood Drives

    GFTW has also hosted American Red Cross blood drives at its facility. In May 2022, the organization hosted a blood drive from 2 to 6 p.m. at 7539 Stage Road, Concord, contributing to local blood supplies while supporting community health.[62]

    Financial Data and Scale

    Gleaning for the World has achieved extraordinary financial scale as a humanitarian organization, distributing enormous quantities of supplies while maintaining exceptional operational efficiency. The organization's growth trajectory demonstrates the effectiveness of its model and the generosity of its donor base.

    Supply Distribution Volume

    By 2011, GFTW had provided approximately half a billion dollars worth of supplies to impoverished individuals globally since its 1998 founding.[8] The organization shipped an estimated $40 to $50 million worth of supplies annually by 2011.[63] By 2006, GFTW had shipped over $300 million worth of supplies to people in need, both within the United States and internationally.[64]

    More recent data indicates that by 2014, the organization had distributed approximately $547 million worth of supplies over its first sixteen years of operation.[65] As of 2024, GFTW has nearly three decades of consistent growth and supply distribution.

    Operational Scale and Shipment Numbers

    The organization has shipped supplies in full tractor-trailer loads on a regular and increasing basis. By 2014, GFTW had achieved its 3,000th shipment milestone, having sent its 3,000th tractor-trailer load in March 2014.[66] In June 2010, the organization had reached its 2,000th shipment milestone with a load of bottled water sent to flood victims.[24] These milestones demonstrate the organization's consistent growth and increasing output over time.

    In 2014, GFTW was sending multiple shipments monthly. In April alone, the organization sent over a dozen shipments to destinations including Latin America, the Caribbean, and Syria.[67] By early 2010, GFTW had doubled the number of shipments compared to the same period in 2009, with shipments sent to Haiti, victims of Alabama and Mississippi flooding, and Guatemala.[68]

    Geographic Reach

    As of 2010, GFTW had provided supplies to people in need in 57 countries.[69] By 2014, the organization had sent supplies to 54 countries across five continents, demonstrating its truly global reach.[10] Typical destination regions have included Latin America, the Caribbean, Southeast Asia, Africa, the Middle East, and Eastern Europe, as well as disaster zones throughout the United States.

    Volunteer Engagement

    GFTW has built a substantial volunteer base to support its operations. By 2014, the organization had approximately 1,820 volunteers working annually at its facilities and in community collection efforts.[70] By 2015, GFTW aimed to expand its volunteer base from 2,100 to 4,000 annually through the opening of a new volunteer center.[15] Volunteers engage in various activities including sorting supplies, packing shipments, processing donations, and coordinating collection drives in the community.

    Efficiency Metrics

    A defining characteristic of GFTW has been its commitment to financial efficiency. In 2012, the organization was recognized for being a top charity in the country for several years, having shipped $40 to $50 million worth of supplies annually while providing half a billion dollars in supplies globally since 1998.[71] The organization's efficiency model ensures that each dollar donated enables approximately $211 worth of supplies to be purchased and distributed.[9]

    GFTW has attributed its exceptional efficiency to its business practices, emphasizing that the entire donation is used to help people with no overhead costs deducted from donor contributions.[72] Despite the challenging economy of 2011, GFTW experienced a 30 percent growth in donations, reflecting donor confidence in the organization's model.[72]
