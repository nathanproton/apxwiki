    Appomattox Christian Academy (ACA) is a private Christian school located in Appomattox County, Virginia, serving students from kindergarten through twelfth grade.[2] Founded in 2005, the institution was established as a response to parental demand for Christian education and serves as Appomattox County's only accredited private school.[1] The academy is affiliated with the Anglican and Reformed Episcopal churches and operates according to a classical Christian educational philosophy.[3] In recent years, ACA has expanded its educational offerings to include a Career and Technical Education (CTE) Institute, which opened in 2023 at the Carver-Price Complex in partnership with Central Virginia Community College.[8]

    History

    Appomattox Christian Academy was founded in 2005 by Christian families in Appomattox County seeking an alternative educational approach for their children.[1] The school's establishment was announced in February 2005 with plans to open in September of that same year for kindergarten through eighth grade.[1] Initially, ACA expected to enroll at least twenty students, though officials anticipated higher enrollment from the outset.[1] The academy was situated in a 10,000-square-foot building located in Appomattox Anglican Church, situated on State Route 635 in the location of the old harp factory behind the Appomattox Rescue Squad building.[1] The facility featured a gymnasium, dining room, chapel, playground, classrooms, and computers in each classroom.[1]

    Rev. Geoffrey C. Hubler was appointed as the founding principal, while Marcie S. Jones served as Head Mistress.[1] The school's initial board of directors included Hubler, Jones, Rich Guild (treasurer), Deborah Powell, and Meredith Boone.[1] From its inception, ACA welcomed students of all Christian denominations, colors, and ethnic backgrounds, emphasizing that while located in an Anglican church building, the school operated as an inclusive community.[1]

    In 2007, the academy appointed Rev. Rodney H. Longmire as headmaster, a significant leadership transition that reflected the school's expansion and evolution.[3] Longmire brought extensive experience in classical Christian education, having served as Warden of New Covenant Schools in Lynchburg and holding the rank of Colonel in the Virginia Defense Force as Chief of Chaplains.[3] Under Longmire's leadership, ACA developed plans for continued expansion, implementing the classical Christian educational model that would characterize the institution's instructional approach.[3]

    Over the following years, ACA expanded its grade offerings and deepened its facilities and programs. By the 2020s, the school had extended its curriculum through twelfth grade and undertook significant capital improvements to support its growing mission.[19] A major renovation and consecration of the school's renovated facilities was held in October 2021, marking another milestone in the institution's development.[19]

    Mission and Academics

    Appomattox Christian Academy operates according to a classical Christian educational philosophy that integrates faith and learning throughout the curriculum.[3] The school's founding mission emphasized that God and God's word should be at the center of children's education, reflecting the Christian parents' perspective that prompted the school's establishment.[1] As a classical Christian institution, ACA emphasizes rigorous academics combined with character development rooted in Christian principles.

    As of 2023, ACA is Appomattox County's only accredited private school, providing an alternative to public school education.[6] The school serves students from kindergarten through twelfth grade, offering a comprehensive curriculum designed to prepare students for higher education and success in their chosen fields. The academy welcomes families seeking educational alternatives and has cultivated a reputation for small class sizes and personalized instruction.

    In February 2023, ACA held an activity-oriented open house event to highlight the school's qualities and demonstrate its educational approach to prospective families.[6] The school's business operations are managed by Patricia Tracey, who emphasizes ACA's role in providing options to Appomattox families in response to various concerns about public school education.[6]

    Career and Technical Education Institute

    In July 2022, the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors voted unanimously to approve a five-year lease agreement allowing ACA to develop a vocational training center at the Carver-Price Complex.[7] The Career and Technical Education (CTE) Institute was conceived to provide students with practical training in high-demand trades including welding, plumbing, electrical skills, and health and human services.[7] The vocational center was designed to benefit not only ACA students but also Appomattox County residents seeking job-ready skills and industry certification.[7]

    In February 2023, U.S. Congressman Bob Good, who represents Virginia's 5th District, toured the future site of the vocational school as it underwent renovations at the Carver-Price Center.[5] ACA developed the educational program in conjunction with Central Virginia Community College (CVCC) Appomattox Center, enabling dual-enrollment opportunities for both ACA and county students.[5] The CTE Institute was scheduled to open in the fall of 2023.[5]

    The CTE Institute received significant support from regional businesses and utilities. In July 2023, Dominion Energy presented a grant to ACA to help fund important equipment for the new center, specifically providing the school's first set of welding booths.[8] According to ACA Board Chairperson Bruce Boone, the welding booths were designed with modern safety and health features that provide cleaner air flow and improved conditions for welding students.[8] The institute was reported to have opened around the last week of August 2023.[8]

    Athletics and Extracurriculars

    Appomattox Christian Academy offers various extracurricular activities and athletic programs to complement its academic curriculum. The school has engaged in community events and fundraising activities that reflect its commitment to service and fellowship.

    The academy hosts several annual community events that have become established traditions. Beginning in the mid-2010s, ACA began hosting Christmas Markets that attracted numerous vendors and community members.[12] Building on the success of six Christmas Markets, ACA expanded into seasonal programming by hosting its first Appomattox Spring Market in March 2022.[11] According to Brandi Tweedy, ACA's director of operations, the spring market was established in response to vendor requests for year-round opportunities and proved popular with both established and new vendors.[11]

    ACA has also been recognized for achievements in academic and cultural competitions. In April 2020, an ACA senior competed in a National Shakespeare Competition, with viewers given the opportunity to vote for audience choice awards.[20] The school has participated in community service initiatives, including a shoe drive benefiting scholarships and international small businesses in need, which was held in December 2021.[17]

    The school holds annual commencement ceremonies celebrating graduating seniors. In June 2022, despite weather challenges including a severe storm warning during the ceremony, ACA held graduation for its two seniors, Orion Houk and Victor Shockley, with the latter honored as valedictorian.[9] In June 2023, the school celebrated a graduating class of four students, with Solomon Gehan crowned valedictorian.[4]

    Facilities and Campus

    Appomattox Christian Academy's main campus is located on Redfields Road in Appomattox County.[11] The original facility, which opened in 2005, was housed in a 10,000-square-foot building shared with Appomattox Anglican Church, featuring a gymnasium, dining room, chapel, playground, and equipped classrooms.[1]

    In 2018, the town council approved a waterline extension to serve the Christian Academy, reflecting municipal investment in the school's infrastructure and future growth.[23] A major renovation and consecration of the school's facilities occurred in October 2021, marking a significant capital improvement project.[19]

    The Career and Technical Education Institute, which opened in 2023, is located at the Carver-Price Complex, a site that had previously housed Appomattox Elementary School and continues to provide space for Central Virginia Community College's Appomattox Center.[5] The complex also houses the Carver-Price Legacy Museum.[5] The CTE facility includes welding equipment and other vocational training resources designed to prepare students for careers in skilled trades.

    Community Involvement

    Appomattox Christian Academy has established itself as an active community institution engaged in various civic and charitable endeavors. The school's location within Appomattox and its mission-driven approach have fostered relationships with local government, businesses, and civic organizations.

    In recognition of outstanding students, Monroe Masonic Lodge #301 of Appomattox has recognized ACA graduates with the Dr. Clyde G. O'Brien Scholarship.[10] Graduating seniors attending Appomattox Christian Academy, along with peers from Appomattox County High School and Cornerstone Christian Academy, have been eligible to apply for the lodge's $1,000 scholarships to support their higher education pursuits.[10]

    The school maintains partnerships with local educational institutions and has integrated itself into the county's educational landscape. ACA's participation in county school choice week and other educational initiatives reflects its commitment to being recognized as a viable educational option for Appomattox families.[22]

    The Spot and Trot Book Bus service, operated through the region's library system, schedules regular visits to Appomattox Christian Academy to provide early literacy support to younger students.[24] This ongoing partnership demonstrates the school's engagement with community literacy initiatives and family support services.
