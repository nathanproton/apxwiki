    Nathan Archer Simpson (born c. 2000–2001) is an American entrepreneur, technology professional, and politician who represents the Courthouse District on the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors. He took office on January 1, 2026, succeeding longtime supervisor Samuel E. “Sammy” Carter, who had held the seat since 1998. Simpson previously served on the Appomattox Town Council from 2020 to 2025 and on the Appomattox County Economic Development Authority (EDA) from 2019 to 2020. He is the founder of Colonial Systems LLC, a media production company that was awarded a $2.1 million contract with the United States Marine Corps Special Operations Command in 2020.[1]

    Simpson first gained public attention as a freshman at Appomattox County High School in 2014, when his design was selected as the school’s new athletic logo.[2] During his time on Town Council, he championed initiatives including town-wide Wi-Fi access, train depot renovation, food truck policies, and competitive staff salaries.[3][4][5]

    Early life and education

    Nathan Archer Simpson grew up in Appomattox, Virginia. He attended Appomattox County High School, where he was active in academic competitions, entrepreneurship programs, and extracurricular activities. Records indicate he was a freshman during the 2013–2014 school year.[2]

    ACHS logo design

    In early 2014, Appomattox County High School launched a contest for a new athletic logo after the school received a cease-and-desist letter from the Collegiate Licensing Corporation on behalf of the University of Arizona, which objected to the school’s “Block A” design as too similar to its own trademarked logo. Athletic Director Chris Dodge solicited submissions from students, parents, staff, alumni, and community members, receiving over forty proposals by the April 25 deadline.[6]

    Simpson, then a freshman, submitted the winning design. In July 2014, the Appomattox County School Board voted 4–1 to adopt his logo, with board member Greg Smith dissenting.[2][7] The new design was phased in across sports apparel, athletic uniforms, scoreboards, and other school property over the following years.

    SkillsUSA and Model United Nations

    Simpson was a member of the Appomattox County High School SkillsUSA Entrepreneurship Team, which won the Virginia state championship in the entrepreneurship category for three consecutive years. In 2018, Simpson, alongside teammates Liam Bennett and Bryce Gunter, captured the team’s third consecutive state gold medal under the guidance of adviser Jason Clark. The team qualified for the SkillsUSA National Conference in Louisville, Kentucky, held June 24–29, 2018. The Appomattox Entrepreneurship Team had also placed at the national competition in each of the previous two years.[8]

    In November 2017, Simpson received the Best Delegation award at a Model United Nations event held at Hampden-Sydney College, where he represented the United States.[9]

    Career

    Town of Appomattox IT work

    In June 2017, while still a high school student, Simpson was hired as a part-time Information Technology employee for the Town of Appomattox. Interim Town Manager Clarence Monday announced the hiring at a Town Council meeting.[10] In that role, Simpson worked on a variety of technology projects for the town, including upgrades to the town’s website and meeting livestream equipment. Town Council members thanked Simpson on multiple occasions for his work on these projects.[11] He also rebuilt the Appomattox Tourism website, HistoricAppomattox.com, launching the updated version in January 2018.[12]

    Colonial Systems LLC

    Simpson founded Colonial Systems LLC, a media production company. In February 2020, the Times-Virginian reported that the company had been awarded a $2.1 million, five-year contract by the United States Marine Corps Special Operations Command (MARSOC) to produce videography and photography for military recruitment advertisements shown in movie theaters. Simpson was nineteen years old at the time of the contract award.[1]

    Economic Development Authority (2019–2020)

    On February 28, 2019, the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors appointed Simpson to fill a vacancy on the Appomattox County Economic Development Authority for a four-year term set to expire in December 2022.[13] Simpson served on the EDA until 2020, when his election to the Appomattox Town Council created a vacancy. The Board of Supervisors appointed Patrick Richardson to fill Simpson’s unexpired EDA term on August 27, 2020.[14]

    Appomattox Town Council (2020–2025)

    Simpson was elected to the Appomattox Town Council on May 19, 2020, as one of six candidates who won seats. The other members elected that cycle were Timothy W. Garrett, Jonathan D. Garrett, Mary Lou Spiggle, Paul D. Harvey, and Claudia G. Puckette.[15] Upon taking office, Mayor Richard Conner appointed Simpson to the Physical Development Committee alongside Timothy Garrett and Mary Lou Spiggle.[16] Simpson also agreed to fill an unexpired term on the Town of Appomattox Planning Commission, serving until June 30, 2022.[17]

    During his five years on Council, Simpson became known for championing technology and economic development initiatives, frequently proposing new programs and raising issues related to town infrastructure and quality of life.

    Wi-Fi and internet access

    At the September 29, 2020 workshop meeting, Simpson initiated a discussion on establishing a town-wide Wi-Fi coverage system. He noted that internet access had become essential for both working adults and students, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic, and asked whether staff or fellow council members knew where previous efforts to develop municipal Wi-Fi had stalled. Fellow council member Paul Harvey suggested inviting B2X Online to present a proposal, as the company had recently pitched an internet plan to the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors.[3]

    Train depot renovation

    Simpson was a persistent advocate for the renovation of the historic Appomattox train depot on Main Street, a project that had languished for years. In October 2020, he placed the train depot renovation on the council agenda and expressed concern about the building’s condition. He stated he had been following the issue and wanted to start a conversation about cost estimates and a path forward.[4]

    The issue resurfaced at an April 2021 workshop meeting, where Simpson joined veteran council members Mary Lou Spiggle and Claudia Puckette in calling for concrete action. Spiggle noted that citizens had commented on the building’s deteriorating exterior, and at a May 2021 meeting Simpson expressed support for forming an exploratory committee.[18][19] The council later issued a request for proposals for engineering and project management services for the depot renovation, and by 2024 the project had advanced to the construction advertisement stage, with the Appomattox County Chamber of Commerce requesting space in the renovated building.[20]

    Food truck policy

    In March 2021, Simpson proposed that the council develop short-term and long-term policies for food trucks operating in the town. He specifically advocated for a pilot food truck park program and supported local entrepreneur Alan Briceland, owner of Iceland’s ice cream and dessert truck, in his plans to expand operations on his own property. Simpson told Briceland at the meeting that the town needed more entrepreneurs, and the council reached a consensus to allow Briceland to move forward with his plans.[5]

    Skateboard ordinance

    In September 2020, the Town Council reviewed a longstanding ordinance that prohibited skateboarding, scooters, roller skating, and bicycling in certain areas of the town. After citizens—both youth and adults—spoke against the ban at the August meeting, the council voted to revise the ordinance by lowering the penalty for violations rather than eliminating the ban entirely. Simpson was the lone dissenting vote, opposing the revision.[21]

    Tourism website

    At a January 2023 workshop meeting, Simpson disclosed that he had rebuilt the Appomattox Tourism website, HistoricAppomattox.com, in 2017–2018 when he was employed as the town’s IT technician. Noting that the site had not been updated since its January 2018 launch, Simpson proposed issuing a Request for Proposal to solicit local bids for a website refresh, stating that a local developer familiar with the town’s tourist attractions would add value.[12]

    Staff salary advocacy

    In November 2021, Simpson publicly advocated for reviewing and adjusting the salaries of all town employees to be competitive with other municipalities. His remarks came after the council approved a salary increase for the Town Manager, and Simpson expressed hope that the raise would set a precedent for compensation reviews across the town’s workforce.[22]

    2024 censure and rescission

    On March 11, 2024, the Appomattox Town Council voted to censure Simpson for alleged “disorderly behavior and misconduct” following an incident in the Town Hall parking lot after the February 27 council meeting.[23] Council members Claudia Puckette and Mary Lou Spiggle reported that after the February meeting, Simpson shouted at them and moved toward them in what they described as a threatening manner, causing them to fear for their personal safety.[24]

    The incident followed a disagreement over the potential appointment of Simpson’s wife to the Economic Development Authority. During a break at the February 27 meeting, Puckette and Spiggle had approached Simpson’s wife to explain that she was not selected for the EDA position due to concerns about a perceived conflict of interest. Simpson’s wife subsequently reported the conversation to Simpson in their vehicle.[24]

    Simpson was not present at the March 11 meeting when the censure was adopted and stated he was not notified beforehand or given an opportunity to present his account of events. A special meeting was called for March 19 to discuss the censure.[25] At a special session on March 20, 2024, the council voted to rescind the censure, with five members voting in favor and one abstaining. Simpson and council member Jane Allen presented a resolution arguing that the censure had violated Robert’s Rules of Order, specifically Rule 61.22, which requires that charges be formally preferred and a trial held before the assembly takes disciplinary action.[24]

    Simpson admitted at the March 20 meeting that he had shouted at Puckette and Spiggle from across the parking lot but denied running toward them, raising his arms, or approaching them in a threatening way. The rescission resolution included an addendum, recommended by Puckette, requiring all council members to complete a training course on professional conduct. Simpson agreed to the addendum, and Mayor Richard Conner urged the council to move forward.[24]

    Board of Supervisors (2026–present)

    In 2025, Simpson was elected to represent the Courthouse District on the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors, succeeding Samuel E. “Sammy” Carter, who had held the seat since 1998. Simpson took office on January 1, 2026.[26]

    Other public service

    In addition to his elected and appointed positions, Simpson served on the Town of Appomattox Physical Development Committee (appointed 2020) and filled an unexpired term on the Town of Appomattox Planning Commission (2020–2022).[16][17]

    Simpson purchased a home in the North Avenue Subdivision in the Town of Appomattox in January 2022.[27]
