    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center is a 4-H youth development facility located in Appomattox County, Virginia, near Holiday Lake State Park within the Appomattox Buckingham State Forest.[1] Founded in 1942, the center serves as a residential and day-use educational venue offering hands-on learning experiences in natural resources, outdoor skills, shooting sports, and other youth development programs to students from 17 surrounding counties and the cities of Lynchburg and Charlottesville.[2] The center operates under the auspices of Virginia 4-H and Virginia Cooperative Extension, making it a cornerstone institution for youth education and leadership development in the central Virginia region.

    History and Founding

    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center was established in 1942 as a regional youth development and education facility serving the central Virginia piedmont.[3] The center's creation reflected a broader commitment by Virginia Cooperative Extension and the 4-H youth development organization to provide experiential learning opportunities for young people across multiple counties. Over the decades, the facility has evolved from a simple camp into a comprehensive educational center offering specialized programming in natural resources, outdoor skills, and youth leadership development.

    The center marked a significant milestone in 2017 when it celebrated its 75th anniversary with a special celebration and ribbon-cutting ceremony on March 30, 2017.[4] This event recognized seven decades of service to youth in the region and honored the contributions of campers throughout the center's history. The 75th anniversary celebration featured lunch and opportunities for attendees to share stories from different decades of the center's operation.

    Location and Geography

    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center is located in Appomattox County, Virginia, situated within the Appomattox Buckingham State Forest and adjacent to Holiday Lake State Park.[5] This strategic location provides direct access to natural resources and outdoor recreational areas essential to the center's educational mission. The proximity to Holiday Lake itself offers opportunities for water-based recreation and aquatic education programs, while the surrounding forest provides habitat for wildlife education and outdoor skills training.

    The center's location places it within the central Virginia region, allowing it to serve as a hub for youth education and programming for 17 surrounding counties as well as the cities of Lynchburg and Charlottesville.[6] This geographic centrality has made the facility an important educational resource for the broader piedmont region of Virginia.

    Facilities and Infrastructure

    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center maintains a comprehensive array of facilities designed to support both residential overnight programs and day-use educational activities. The center features multiple cabins for overnight campers, allowing groups of youth to experience residential camping and community living.[7] These accommodations have been regularly upgraded and maintained to ensure safety and comfort for visiting youth groups.

    A notable feature of the facility is its swimming pool, which serves as both a recreational amenity and an educational resource for aquatic safety and swimming instruction.[8] The construction of the pool involved significant community fundraising efforts, including a notable raffle in 1983 where Ginny Sweet, an eight-year-old from Appomattox, won a 1983 Chevrolet Chevette as a grand prize to support the pool fundraiser.[9] The pool remains an integral part of the center's recreational programming and is a popular feature during hot summer days, attracting youth participants to the facility.

    The center is situated directly adjacent to Holiday Lake itself, which provides opportunities for fishing education, boating instruction, and water-based nature study.[10] Various activity areas throughout the grounds support hands-on education in outdoor skills, from archery ranges to nature exploration areas.

    In 2024, the center underwent significant infrastructure improvements with the completion of a new pavilion structure, enhancing its capacity to host larger groups and community events.[11] This addition reflects the center's continued commitment to improving its facilities and expanding programming capacity.

    Educational Programs

    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center operates one of the most comprehensive natural resource education programs in Virginia. The Natural Resource Education Program serves over 4,000 students annually from schools throughout the center's 17-county service area and the cities of Lynchburg and Charlottesville.[12] This program offers both in-school educational experiences and field-based instruction focused on earth and life sciences aligned with Virginia Standards of Learning.

    The center's educational programming expanded significantly in recent years. Between Fall 2013 and Fall 2014, the center increased its natural resource education sessions from 17 to 37 different offerings, demonstrating growing demand for environmental education services.[13] This expansion reflects both the quality of programming and the recognition by schools and educators of the value provided by the center's hands-on approach to science education.

    Educational programming focuses on practical, experiential learning in key subject areas including life science, wildlife management, earth science, ecology, and outdoor skills. Rather than traditional classroom instruction, the center emphasizes outdoor classrooms and field experiences that allow students to directly observe and interact with natural systems. Programs are designed to meet educational standards while fostering environmental stewardship and outdoor competency in young people.

    Shooting Sports and Hunter Education

    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center has become renowned throughout Virginia for its comprehensive shooting sports and hunter education programs. The center hosts the Virginia Hunter Skills Weekends program, which was established in 2009 to provide advanced training for graduating hunter education students and adult hunters seeking to develop specialized skills.[14]

    The Hunter Skills Weekend program offers instruction in multiple disciplines including rifle shooting, shotgun marksmanship, bow hunting, muzzleloading, and survival skills.[15] These weekends typically run for three days and are designed for participants aged 11 and up, with adult participation strongly encouraged. The program combines instruction in hunting techniques with game processing and wild game cooking, providing comprehensive education in responsible hunting practices.

    In 2014, the center hosted its first Hunter Skills Weekend of the year with 65 participants, demonstrating significant regional interest in these specialized programs.[16] Subsequent Hunter Skills Weekends have included diverse offerings such as bow tuning instruction, GPS navigation for hunting, techniques for recovering wounded game, and shotgun basics, each session tailored to meet evolving student needs and interests.

    The center also hosts the Virginia 4-H Shooting Sports State Shoot, a major statewide competition drawing hundreds of competitors from across Virginia in various shooting disciplines including rifle, shotgun, air pistol, and archery.[17] In 2014, the Virginia 4-H State Shoot attracted 300 participants from around the region to compete at the center.[18] By 2019, the state shoot had grown to attract over 325 competitors, making it one of the largest 4-H shooting sports competitions in the state.

    The center has also become an important venue for shooting education instructor training. In October 2017, the center hosted the Virginia 4-H Shooting Education Instructor Certification Training, which brought together 83 volunteer adults and teens from across Virginia to become certified instructors in disciplines including archery, rifle, shotgun, and pistol shooting.[19]

    Specialized hunting workshops are regularly offered at the facility. The center has hosted decoy carving workshops for duck hunters, traditional flintlock rifle building workshops, and waterfowl management seminars.[20] These specialized workshops attract participants from across the region and beyond, with many enrolling for the instructional expertise, residential experience, and camaraderie the center provides.

    Summer and Specialized Camps

    Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center operates several signature residential camp programs serving different age groups and interests throughout the year. The Cloverbud Camp serves the youngest 4-H participants, ages 5-8, introducing them to camping, nature exploration, swimming, and group activities in a supportive residential environment.[21] In its first documented summer in 2018, Cloverbud Camp introduced over 115 young campers to outdoor recreation and camping experiences.

    The center offers a full-week summer camp program for youth ages 9-13, typically held in July and August. These camps feature morning classes in various subjects including outdoor skills, nature study, crafts, sports, and horsemanship, with afternoons dedicated to recreational activities like swimming, canoeing, and hiking, and evenings featuring special programs and entertainment.[22]

    Specialized youth camps have included the Fishing Camp, designed for ages 9-13, which provides instruction in knot tying, casting techniques, fish identification, fishing safety, equipment use, and fish cleaning.[23] Fishing Camps are typically held in spring and have become popular offerings with limited capacity due to their specialized nature.

    Dance Camp, held in September and open to youth ages 5-13, offers instruction in various dance styles from university-level dance instructors, providing cultural enrichment and physical fitness in a fun, supportive setting.[24]

    Camp Rock provides geology-focused environmental education for elementary and middle school youth, featuring rock and fossil identification, gold panning, mineral study, and earth science principles applied to real-world exploration.[25]

    The Teen Retreat Weekend, designed for ages 13-18, offers a weekend camp experience featuring fun classes, campfires, outdoor activities, and social programming like costume parties, designed to build leadership and friendship among older youth.[26]

    Christmas Camp, held in December and themed "Going Green," features holiday crafts, cookie baking, winter nature hikes, talent shows, recycling education, and bingo for youth ages 5-13, combining holiday traditions with environmental learning.[27]

    The center has also offered Family Camp programs featuring classes for family bonding along with individual experiences in swimming, canoeing, theater arts, nature study, and archery.[28]

    Workshops and Skill-Building

    Beyond traditional camps, Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center hosts numerous specialized workshops attracting adults and youth seeking to develop specific skills. Oil painting workshops, led by Certified Ross Instructor Judy Moore-Gist, have been regularly offered, teaching the wet-on-wet painting technique popularized by television artist Bob Ross.[29] These workshops typically run for one day and serve ages 15 and up, including beginners, with instruction, materials, and lunch included in registration fees.

    Fishing rod building workshops, taught by expert craftsman Ron Walters, have introduced participants to the art of constructing custom fly rods and fishing rods, combining craftsmanship with practical outdoor skills.[30]

    Banjo and mandolin building workshops for adults have been led by master craftsman Don Kawalek, providing beginner-level instruction in building stringed instruments from kits and developing understanding of instrument construction and finishing techniques.[31]

    Weaving workshops have been offered, taught by expert weaver Patricia Morton, focusing on basic weaving skills using rigid heddle looms and other traditional weaving equipment, with participants completing projects suitable for display or use.[32]

    Knitting classes held throughout October have taught basic stitches, casting on and off, color changes, and pattern work, with participants completing hats and scarves during the course.[33]

    Specialized workshops have included a Primitive Bow Making Workshop covering four days of instruction in historical bow construction, arrow shaft crafting, and string making with limited enrollment to ensure quality instruction.

    The center has hosted a Women's Retreat featuring multiple class sessions in areas such as crafts, canning, exercise, couponing, automotive skills, bread baking, and rifle instruction, along with gift auctions and social activities.[34]

    Women's Outdoor Weekends have provided classes in outdoor skills and activities for women and girls ages 10 and up, sponsored in partnership with Wilderness Discovery.[35]

    Connection to Virginia Tech and Cooperative Extension

    Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center operates as a facility of Virginia Cooperative Extension, the statewide network providing research-based education to Virginia's agricultural, business, and household communities. The center represents one of Cooperative Extension's most significant facilities dedicated to youth development and environmental education.

    The connection to Virginia Tech, which administers the Cooperative Extension program across Virginia, ensures that programming at the center reflects current research in environmental science, youth development, and outdoor education. Faculty and specialists from Virginia Tech and Virginia State University have provided instructional support for various programs at the center, including sessions in the Natural Resource Education Program.

    The Natural Resource Education Program's alignment with Virginia Standards of Learning ensures that student experiences at the center contribute directly to educational standards in life science, earth science, and environmental literacy. This integration of research-based knowledge with experiential outdoor learning distinguishes the center's programming from traditional classroom instruction.

    Leadership and Staff

    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center has been led by dedicated administrators and staff members committed to youth development and environmental education. Preston Willson served as Park President and CEO during the 2010s, overseeing major fundraising initiatives and grant acquisitions for the center.[36] Willson was instrumental in securing significant funding for the center's natural resource education program expansion.

    Brenda Goin served as Fiscal Coordinator at the center for 28 years, retiring in December 2017 after decades of dedicated service managing the center's financial operations and administrative functions.[37]

    Don Franklin Jimison served as manager of Holiday Lake 4-H Education Center for 10 years, managing day-to-day operations and contributing significantly to the center's programming and facility maintenance.[38] Prior to his tenure at the center, Jimison had served as a basketball coach, teacher, and school principal before moving to Appomattox in 1975.

    Dr. Kenneth Powell served as Chairman of the Holiday Lake 4-H Center Fund Raising Drive, coordinating major capital campaign efforts to support facility improvements and programming expansion.[39]

    The center employs seasonal instructors, counselors, and program coordinators who deliver specialized programming in shooting sports, natural resources, outdoor skills, and youth development activities. Many instructors bring expertise from professional backgrounds or advanced training in their subject areas.

    Renovations and Capital Improvements

    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center has undergone numerous renovations and facility improvements since its founding in 1942. Construction of the swimming pool in 2012 represented a major capital improvement, with concrete being poured for the new facility in April 2012 as the community and center worked toward completion.[48]

    In 2017, a significant renovation project resulted in the completion of an improved Girls' Bathhouse, marked by an official ribbon-cutting ceremony held March 30, 2017, as part of the center's 75th anniversary celebration.[49] This facility upgrade improved sanitary facilities and accommodations for female campers and visitors.

    In 2024, the center completed construction of a new pavilion structure, expanding its capacity to host larger groups and outdoor community events.[50] This addition represents continued investment in the facility's infrastructure and capacity to serve the region.

    The Appomattox County Board of Supervisors unanimously supported a resolution requesting $332,000 in state capital improvement funding for the Holiday Lake 4-H Education Center in October 2019.[51] These requested funds were designated for essential safety upgrades and facility improvements, including designating the center as an evacuation site for county students in emergency situations.

    The center has maintained its facilities through routine maintenance and preventive care. In 2005, Holiday Lake was drained for significant repairs and beach maintenance work, during which fish attractors and underwater structures were placed to improve habitat and fish populations.[52]

The $10,000 grant allowed the center to expand participation in its Natural Resource Education Program, enabling over 650 students to benefit from the hands-on science curriculum aligning with the foundation's STEM education initiatives.

    The Timken Company awarded a $2,000 grant to support the Holiday Lake 4-H Center's Natural Resource Education Program in 2015, enabling 100 elementary and middle school students from Campbell County to participate in nature programs.[55]

    Wells Fargo Bank awarded an additional $10,000 grant in 2016 to support the Natural Resource Education Program, recognizing the program's alignment with Virginia Standards of Learning and its importance to central Virginia youth education.[56]

    Local service organizations have contributed significantly to the center's support. The Appomattox Lions Club presented a $2,000 check to Dr. Kenneth Powell, Chairman of the Holiday Lake 4-H Center Fund Raising Drive, in June 2014, demonstrating strong community organizational support.[57]

    The Appomattox Extension Homemakers Council donated to the 4-H Education Center, with Mrs. Geraldine Marshall presenting a check on behalf of the organization to Mr. Preston Wilson, president of the 4-H Education Center at Holiday Lake, in 1982.[58]

    J.E. Sears & Co. presented a donation to the Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center, with Robert Sears making the presentation to Frank Sweet, 4-H Extension Agent, in 1982.[59]

    The Appomattox County Board of Supervisors has provided direct funding support. In 2007, the Board of Supervisors voted unanimously to donate $10,000 to the Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center to support its maintenance fund, with the donation intended as the first of several annual contributions from the 17 counties served by the center.[60]

The Virginia 4-H Shooting Sports State Shoot, held at Holiday Lake 4-H Center, has grown from 300 competitors in 2014 to over 325 competitors by 2019, demonstrating the expanding appeal and importance of the facility for statewide youth development programming.

    Appomattox County and surrounding areas support vibrant 4-H programs with youth participating in Cloverbud programs for younger children (ages 5-8), traditional 4-H programs for school-age youth, and shooting sports and natural resources programs for diverse age groups and interests.

    Community Impact and Legacy

    The Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center stands as a significant institution in central Virginia, having served youth development and environmental education for over eight decades. The center's impact extends far beyond Appomattox County to 17 surrounding counties and the cities of Lynchburg and Charlottesville, making it a regional hub for hands-on youth education.

    The center's natural resource education program, serving over 4,000 students annually, directly supports Virginia Standards of Learning and contributes measurably to the environmental and scientific literacy of central Virginia youth. By bringing thousands of students outdoors for direct interaction with natural systems, the center fosters environmental stewardship and scientific understanding in ways that traditional classroom instruction cannot achieve.

    The center's shooting sports programs have established it as the premier venue in central Virginia for hunter education, shooting sports competitions, and firearms safety training. By hosting the Virginia Hunter Skills Weekends and the Virginia 4-H Shooting Sports State Shoot, the facility has become essential infrastructure for responsible hunting and shooting sports in the state.

    As a residential and day-use facility accommodating hundreds of youth weekly throughout the year, the center provides experiential learning opportunities that build character, develop outdoor competencies, foster leadership, and create lasting friendships among participants. For many young people in the region, participation in programs at Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center represents transformative educational experiences that shape their understanding of the natural world and their own capabilities.

    The center's opening to community organizations for reunions, celebrations, and special events has also strengthened its role as a community gathering place. From class reunions to family events, the facility has become a destination for creating memories and strengthening community bonds across generations.

    Looking forward, the Holiday Lake 4-H Educational Center continues to evolve, with recent facility improvements including the 2024 pavilion construction demonstrating sustained commitment to expanding capacity and enhancing programming. As demand for outdoor education, environmental literacy, and youth development programs continues to grow, the center remains positioned as a vital educational and recreational resource for central Virginia.
