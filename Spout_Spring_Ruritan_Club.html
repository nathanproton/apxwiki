Spout Spring Ruritan Club is a civic service organization located in the Spout Spring community of Appomattox County, Virginia.[1] Founded on May 9, 1966, as a chapter of Ruritan National, the club has served as a major contributor to community development and charitable giving in Appomattox County for nearly six decades.[2] The organization operates a modern clubhouse facility (constructed 1994) and softball field (constructed 1990), which serve as gathering spaces for community events, fundraisers, and youth recreation.[3] Through its annual giving of over $200,000 to local causes and its distribution of $25,000+ in scholarships to area students, the club has established itself as a cornerstone of civic engagement in the region.[4]

History and Founding

The Spout Spring Ruritan Club was chartered on May 9, 1966, as a local chapter of Ruritan National, a nationwide civic and service organization dedicated to community betterment.[5] The club is named for its location in the Spout Spring community of Appomattox County. By the early 1980s, the organization had established itself as an active institution within the county, with documented leadership and ongoing community programs.[6]

Among the club's early founding figures was Edward Nathaniel "Joe" Fuller, III, who was instrumental in building the club's primary facility and served as District Governor for the Appomattox District of Ruritan International.[7] Fuller also initiated the club's successful Tuesday and Friday evening Bingo fundraising program in the early 1980s, which became a major revenue source for community charitable activities.[8]

Facilities and Grounds

The Spout Spring Ruritan Club is located on U.S. Route 460 (Richmond Highway) in the Spout Spring community of Appomattox County.[16] The club operates as an official polling location for county and local elections.[17]

Clubhouse (Constructed 1994)

The club's primary facility is a modern two-story clubhouse completed in 1994, which includes multiple functional areas designed to serve community organizations and events.[18] The clubhouse features a full dining hall and commercial kitchen facilities, multiple meeting rooms suitable for group gatherings, a recreation and multipurpose area, a fitness area, modern bathrooms and restroom facilities, and an outdoor deck.[19] The facility's size and amenities make it suitable for hosting community events, fundraisers, and large gatherings.

Outdoor Facilities

The club maintains a softball field that was constructed in 1990 to provide recreational space for community youth.[20] The grounds also feature playground equipment including swings and other recreational apparatus, as well as outdoor space suitable for events.[21]

Community Use

The clubhouse and grounds serve a diverse range of community organizations and events, including Relay for Life fundraising events and cook-offs, Bingo fundraisers for charitable causes, youth sports events such as Dixie Youth baseball and softball tournaments, elections as an official polling location, fire department events and auxiliary gatherings, business meetings and conferences, church and religious organization events, and regional dog show events such as United Kennel Club Championship Regional Qualifying events.[22] The multipurpose space is also available for events sponsored by the Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT) and Norfolk & Southern Railroad.[23]

Community Service and Charitable Giving

The Spout Spring Ruritan Club is recognized as one of Appomattox County's largest annual donors to community causes, contributing over $200,000 annually to local charitable organizations and public service agencies.[24] The club's charitable work spans multiple areas including emergency services, youth programs, education, health initiatives, and community infrastructure development.

Emergency Services Support

The club has been a consistent supporter of local fire and rescue services. In 1997, the club presented a landmark $25,000 donation to the Appomattox County Volunteer Rescue Squad, presented by Club President Bobby Layne to Treasurer Aileen Ferguson on March 21, 1997.[25] The club also provides annual donations to the Gladstone and Toga Volunteer Fire Departments and supports fire department auxiliary fundraising events.[26] Additionally, the club has contributed to brush truck purchases by the Appomattox County Volunteer Fire Department.[27]

Hunting and Food Assistance

The club annually donates $2,000 to the Hunters for the Hungry program, which provides wild game to county food pantries and assists families in need.[28]

Veteran and Civic Organizations

In 1987, the club partnered with American Legion Post 104 to donate funds to the Post's "Eternal Flame Fund." Norman Ferguson represented both the American Legion and the Spout Spring Ruritan Club in coordinating this donation on September 19, 1987.[29]

Youth and Education

The club provides financial support for Appomattox Primary School community projects and school facility improvements.[30]

Community Parks and Recreation

The club has been a major fundraising partner for the Appomattox Community Center and Appomattox County Parks and Recreation. In 2009-2010, the club pledged $40,000 in matching funds to support the construction of a community park concession stand and press box.[31] The resulting facility, completed in 2011, is a two-story 32-foot-by-32-foot structure that serves as a press box for the community park ballfields and includes restrooms and storage facilities.[32] In 2017, the club committed additional funds to a $137,664 stadium lights project for the upper baseball fields.[33]

Literacy and Community Development

The club provides financial support for literacy initiatives, including donations to the Appomattox Literacy and Intervention Project (ALIP), the "Spot and Trot" book bus programs, and Little Free Libraries at local churches.[34]

Health and Wellness

The club is a regular supporter of the American Cancer Society and hosts annual Relay for Life events at its facility through the "Cindy's Country Cook-Off" fundraiser, a memorial to Cindy Bollinger.[35] The club also supports cystic fibrosis foundation fundraising events.[36]

Scholarship Program

The Spout Spring Ruritan Club administers the Advanced Education Scholarship Program, providing financial assistance to local students pursuing higher education.[37] The program is designed to support residents of Appomattox County who have graduated from Appomattox County High School and are currently attending college, university, technical, or business school.[38]

Eligibility and Award Criteria

Scholarships are awarded based on multiple criteria including financial need, academic achievement, extracurricular involvement, community volunteerism, job experience, and overall merit and character.[39] Applications are submitted through the email address SSRCAdvEdScholarship@gmail.com, with an annual application deadline of April 28 and award notification by May 31.[40] Scholarship decisions are made by a committee and are final.

Recent Awards

In 2023, the club awarded $25,000 in scholarships to 12 local high school seniors, providing awards ranging from $1,000 to $3,000 per student.[41] The club also provides scholarships to students attending the Central Virginia Community College Appomattox satellite branch for vocational and technical education programs.[42] The scholarship program represents a significant portion of the club's $200,000+ annual charitable giving.

Fundraising Activities

The Spout Spring Ruritan Club supports its annual charitable giving through a diverse range of fundraising activities, combining traditional events with modern business partnerships.

Bingo Programs

The club's primary ongoing fundraiser is its Bingo program, initiated in the early 1980s by Joe Fuller as Tuesday and Friday evening events.[43] The program has remained successful as a major revenue source throughout the club's history and continues into the 2020s, supporting various community causes including the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation and local nonprofits.[44] During the COVID-19 pandemic (2020-2021), Bingo attendance restrictions impacted fundraising capacity, and the club could only provide 50% of needed funds for the Habitat fireworks project in 2021.[45]

Cooking Competitions and Cook-Offs

The club hosts annual cooking competitions and cook-offs to raise funds for community causes. The "Cindy's Country Cook-Off," held in partnership with live entertainment from professional musicians and bands such as Shakygrass, serves as a major fundraiser for the American Cancer Society and Relay for Life initiatives.[46]

Special Events and Community Fundraisers

The club organizes and sponsors additional fundraising events including Music Fest events hosted in partnership with local fire departments, Longaberger Basket and 31 Bingo events, and Raider Bingo Committee fundraisers.[47]

Business Associate Member Program

In 2022, the club launched a Business Associate Member program to encourage local business participation in community support efforts.[48] Recent business members joining the program include Hometown Pharmacy (a full-service pharmacy with 20 years of service in Appomattox County), Rehab Associates of Central Virginia, Edward Jones financial services, Appomattox Outdoor Equipment, and Village Print Shop.[49]
